## The Problem

Most mood-tracking apps reduce a day down to an emoji and a streak counter. There's little that makes the act of reflecting feel worth returning to. Innerverse was built as a founder product to explore whether mood tracking could feel more like exploring a personal universe than filling out a form.

## What I Built

Innerverse is a React Native + Expo app that renders each logged mood as a point in a personal "cosmos" — a Skia-driven visualization that grows and shifts as the user logs more entries over time. An AI guide, Nova, built on the OpenAI API, helps the user reflect on patterns in their mood history through conversation rather than static charts. Firebase handles auth, data storage, and the backend for Nova's context.

I designed, built, and published the app solo under Jafrix Systems, my own studio, and it's live on the Play Store.

## Key Challenges

Skia gave the visual flexibility needed for the cosmos metaphor, but rendering a growing, animated point cloud smoothly on lower-end Android devices took real tuning — batching draw calls and being disciplined about what re-renders on each new mood entry. On the AI side, keeping Nova's responses grounded in the user's actual mood history (rather than generic wellness advice) required careful prompt construction using the user's own logged data as context.

## Results

Innerverse is live on the Play Store with growing organic downloads, built and published solo end to end — product design, engineering, and App Store submission.

## Stack Decisions

Expo was chosen to keep iteration speed high as a solo founder, with Skia used specifically for the custom cosmos rendering that off-the-shelf charting libraries couldn't produce. Firebase kept backend overhead minimal so the majority of time could go into the product experience itself rather than infrastructure.
