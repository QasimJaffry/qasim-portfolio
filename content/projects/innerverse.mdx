## The Problem

Most mood apps reduce a day to an emoji and a streak. There’s little reason to come back except guilt. Innerverse asks whether reflection can feel like exploring a personal universe instead of filling a form.

## What I Built

Innerverse is a solo founder product under **Jafrix Systems**: React Native + Expo with a **Skia** cosmos — each logged mood becomes a star in a pinch/pan galaxy that grows over time.

- Celestial mood logging (intensity, tags, notes) and trends/streaks.
- **Mira**, an AI companion grounded in the user’s own mood history (not generic wellness copy).
- Meditation library, Mindscape builder, export (JSON/CSV), offline indicators, and reminder push.
- RevenueCat for premium themes / subscriptions.

Live on [Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=innerverse.app) and [innerverse.app](https://innerverse.app). Source: [GitHub](https://github.com/QasimJaffry/Innerverse).

## Major Features

- **Cosmos journal** — moods as stars in a Skia galaxy, not a spreadsheet.
- **Mira AI** — conversational insights tied to real logged entries.
- **Trends & rituals** — analytics, streaks, meditation, Mindscape.
- **Premium path** — RevenueCat subscriptions and themes.
- **Solo ship** — design, engineering, and store submission end to end.

## Key Challenges

Skia gave the visual freedom the metaphor needed, but a growing animated point cloud on lower-end Android required disciplined redraws. Keeping Mira grounded in the user’s data — not generic advice — meant prompt construction always carried real mood history as context.

## Results

Innerverse is live on Play Store and web, built and published solo — product, engineering, and store listing.

## Stack Decisions

Expo kept solo iteration fast; Skia was required for a custom cosmos no chart library could fake. Firebase minimized backend work so time stayed on the experience; RevenueCat handles store billing without hand-rolled receipts.
