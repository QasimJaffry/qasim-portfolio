## The Problem

Kitty Nip already had an audience as a location-based cat social / dating-style app — but needed an engineer to own the full stack and keep it stable as the product grew past early stage.

## What I Built

I took over **full product ownership**: React Native client plus Firebase (Auth, Firestore, Storage, Functions, Messaging, Analytics, Crashlytics).

- Geo discovery and swipe matching with location/discovery settings.
- Cat and human profiles; photo galleries (premium unlocks richer media).
- Private chat with photos/videos after match; push for matches and messages.
- Premium via IAP; Google Mobile Ads; report flows; Google/Apple sign-in.

Live on [Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=computer.star.kittynip) and [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/kitty-nip-cat-dating-app/id1520805359).

## Major Features

- **Swipe + location discovery** — match with nearby cat people / profiles.
- **Profiles & media** — cat-forward identity with gallery and premium photo rules.
- **Private chat** — post-match messaging with media and push.
- **Monetization** — IAP premium and ads without a separate backend rewrite.
- **Ownership** — client + Firebase backend ops under one engineer.

## Key Challenges

Taking over a live codebase meant stabilizing Firebase data and auth flows before feature work — real users were already depending on chat and matching. Location queries and media-heavy chat needed careful indexing and storage habits as the base grew.

## Results

Kitty Nip stayed live on iOS and Android under my ownership, past **10,000+** Play Store downloads, with backend responsibility included in that scope.

## Stack Decisions

The existing React Native + Firebase stack was retained rather than rewritten — a live social app doesn’t justify rewrite risk. Redux Toolkit and native IAP/ads plugins stayed aligned with what the stores and users already expected.
